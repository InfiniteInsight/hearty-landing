I've been trying to figure out for years whether certain foods are messing with me. Bloating after meals, energy crashes in the afternoon, headaches that seem to come from nowhere. The usual suspects — dairy, gluten, caffeine — don't tell the whole story. The patterns are subtle and they take time to emerge.

The obvious solution: keep a food journal. Track what you eat, track how you feel, let the data speak.

The problem: every app I tried made it too hard.

Some want you to log everything in grams. Some have a database of three million foods but none of them are the thing your partner actually cooked. Some bury symptom tracking three menus deep. After a week, I'd stop — not because I didn't care, but because the friction wasn't worth it.

So I started building Hearty.

## The core idea

The key insight is that **the logging has to be zero-friction**. Not low-friction — zero. If I have to search a database or fill out a form, I won't do it consistently. And consistency is everything when you're trying to find patterns.

So Hearty is voice-first. You say "had oatmeal with blueberries and coffee this morning" and that's it. Hearty parses the structure, fills in what it knows, and flags what it doesn't. The entry is logged.

A short while after you eat, Hearty checks in: *How's your stomach?* A one-sentence reply is enough. Over time, those check-ins build a dataset that an AI can actually reason over.

## What I'm building toward

The goal isn't just a journal — it's a personal health intelligence tool. One that can say, with honest confidence, "dairy seems to correlate with your bloating about two hours later" based on *your* data, not generic dietary advice.

I want it to be the kind of tool I actually use. Minimal interface, maximum insight, completely private.
